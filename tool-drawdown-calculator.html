What is maximum drawdown and why does it matter? ‚ñº

Maximum drawdown (MDD) is the largest peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value before a new peak is reached. It measures the worst loss an investor would have experienced. Formula: MDD = (Trough Value - Peak Value) / Peak Value √ó 100. It matters because deep drawdowns require exponentially larger gains to recover and can psychologically destroy traders.

Why does a 50% loss require a 100% gain to recover? ‚ñº

If your portfolio drops from ‚Çπ100 to ‚Çπ50 (-50%), you now need to gain ‚Çπ50 on a ‚Çπ50 base, which is 100% return. The math is asymmetric: losses hurt more than equivalent gains help. This is why capital preservation is more important than chasing returns.

What is an acceptable maximum drawdown? ‚ñº

Conservative portfolios: 10-15% max DD. Moderate portfolios: 15-25% max DD. Aggressive portfolios: 25-40% max DD. Anything beyond 50% is catastrophic and often unrecoverable psychologically. Professional traders typically have 20% max DD limits before mandatory position reduction.

How can I reduce maximum drawdown? ‚ñº

1) Strict stop losses on all positions 2) Reduce position sizes 3) Diversify across uncorrelated assets 4) Use trailing stops to lock profits 5) Cut exposure during high volatility periods 6) Keep 20-30% cash reserve 7) Never let single position exceed 5% of portfolio. Drawdown management is more important than return maximization.

How long does recovery typically take? ‚ñº

Depends on drawdown severity and market conditions. 20% drawdown: 3-6 months. 40% drawdown: 1-2 years. 60% drawdown: 3-5 years. During secular bear markets like 2000-2002, recovery can take 5-15 years. This is why preventing large drawdowns is critical - you lose years of compounding opportunity.

Should I average down during a drawdown? ‚ñº

Only if: 1) You have predetermined plan 2) Position size is small 3) Thesis is still intact 4) Market structure hasn't broken. Never average down into falling knife momentum stocks or during panic capitulation. Most "average down" strategies turn small losses into catastrophic ones. When in doubt, reduce risk instead of adding.
