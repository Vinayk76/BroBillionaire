Win Rate Myth
High win rate doesn't guarantee profits. 90% win rate with 1:10 R:R loses money. 30% win rate with 1:5 R:R makes money. Focus on expectancy, not win rate alone.
üí∞
Expectancy Formula
Expectancy = (Win% √ó Avg Win) - (Loss% √ó Avg Loss). Positive expectancy = profitable system. Your expectancy per trade is what you make on average over time.
üìä
Profit Factor
Profit Factor = Gross Profit / Gross Loss. Above 2.0 = excellent system. 1.5-2.0 = good. Below 1.5 = needs work. Anything under 1.0 = losing system.
üé≤
Sample Size Matters
Need minimum 30 trades for basic validity, 100+ for statistical significance. 10 winning trades doesn't prove anything - could be luck. Let your edge play out over hundreds of trades.

Risk-Reward Balance
Higher win rate requires smaller R:R. Lower win rate needs larger R:R. Breakeven win rate = 1/(1+R:R). With 1:2 R:R, you need 33.3% win rate to breakeven.
üìà
Consistency > Returns
Consistent 1% daily edge beats volatile 10% weekly edge. Track your standard deviation. Low volatility + positive edge = sustainable long-term profits.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good win rate for trading? ‚ñº

There's no "good" win rate in isolation. Day traders: 50-60%. Swing traders: 40-50%. Trend followers: 30-40%. What matters is expectancy. A 35% win rate with 3:1 R:R is better than 65% win rate with 1:2 R:R. Focus on expectancy per trade, not win percentage.

What is expectancy and why does it matter? ‚ñº

Expectancy is your average profit per trade over time. Formula: (Win% √ó Avg Win) - (Loss% √ó Avg Loss). Positive expectancy = profitable system long-term. ‚Çπ500 expectancy over 100 trades = ‚Çπ50,000 profit. This is THE most important metric for traders.

How do I improve my profit factor? ‚ñº

1) Cut losers faster (reduce average loss) 2) Let winners run longer (increase average win) 3) Filter out low-quality setups (increase win rate) 4) Improve entry timing (better R:R). Small improvements in each area compound dramatically. A 10% improvement in avg win/loss ratio can double profitability.

How many trades do I need to validate my strategy? ‚ñº

Minimum 30 trades for basic idea, 100+ for statistical significance, 300+ for high confidence. Remember: even losing strategies can have 10-trade winning streaks (probability). Don't overtrade to hit sample size - quality over quantity. Track performance in rolling 30-trade windows.

What's the relationship between win rate and risk:reward? ‚ñº

Inverse relationship. Higher win rate = lower R:R acceptable. Lower win rate = higher R:R required. Breakeven win rate = 1/(1+R:R). Examples: 1:1 R:R needs 50% win rate. 1:2 R:R needs 33% win rate. 1:3 R:R needs 25% win rate. Pick your style and optimize accordingly.

Should I track breakeven trades separately? ‚ñº

Yes. Breakeven trades are actually slight losses (commissions + slippage + opportunity cost). Too many breakeven trades signals: 1) Stops too tight 2) No conviction 3) Early exits. Aim for under 10% breakeven rate. Every breakeven trade is a wasted opportunity and mental energy.
