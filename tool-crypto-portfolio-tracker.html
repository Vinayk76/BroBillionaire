Use portfolio trackers to automate calculations and get real-time insights.

What is a good crypto portfolio allocation for Indian investors?

Conservative allocation (Low risk): Bitcoin 50%, Ethereum 30%, Top 10 altcoins 15%, Stablecoins 5%. Moderate allocation (Balanced): Bitcoin 40%, Ethereum 30%, Top 10 altcoins 20%, Mid-cap tokens 10%. Aggressive allocation (High risk): Bitcoin 30%, Ethereum 25%, Top 10 altcoins 25%, Small-cap/DeFi 20%. Key principles for 2026: 1) Bitcoin as foundation (30-50%), 2) Ethereum for smart contracts (25-30%), 3) Never allocate >10% to single altcoin, 4) Keep 5-10% stablecoins for buying dips, 5) Total crypto should be <10% of overall investment portfolio. Rebalance when any asset deviates >15% from target.

How to calculate crypto profit/loss in INR for Indian tax purposes?

Crypto P&L calculation for Indian taxes: 1) Short-term gains (<12 months): Profit = (Selling Price - Purchase Price) √ó Quantity, taxed at 30% flat rate under Section 115BBH, 2) Long-term gains (>12 months): Same 30% tax applies - no LTCG benefit, 3) Always calculate in INR on transaction date using actual exchange rates, 4) FIFO method (First In First Out) for cost basis if multiple purchases, 5) Include transaction fees, GST (1%) as part of cost, 6) No set-off of losses allowed against other income, 7) VDA losses can offset only VDA gains in same year. Maintain detailed records: date, quantity, buy price, sell price, fees, INR conversion. Use crypto tax calculators or consult CA for complex cases with multiple exchanges and wallets.

When to take profit from crypto portfolio - sell all or partial?

Smart profit-taking strategy for 2026: Never sell everything - crypto is long-term play. Use tiered approach: 1) At 50% gain: Sell 20-25% to recover initial investment (now playing with house money), 2) At 100% gain: Sell another 25-30%, let rest run, 3) At 200%+ gain: Sell 30-40%, keep core position (20-25% of original) long-term. Altcoin strategy (more volatile): Take 50% profits at 100% gain, sell 75% at 200%+, keep small position for moonshot. Bitcoin/Ethereum (hodl assets): Never sell core position, only trim 10-20% at major resistance levels. Trigger points: Sell when portfolio becomes >15% of net worth, during extreme greed (RSI >80 on weekly), before known major events. Rebalancing: Monthly review - if any coin >2x target allocation, take partial profits. Don't regret selling - securing profits is never wrong!

How much crypto should be in overall investment portfolio in India 2026?

Crypto allocation in total portfolio: Conservative investors (age 40+, low risk tolerance): 2-5% in crypto (primarily Bitcoin/Ethereum). Moderate investors (age 30-40, medium risk): 5-10% in crypto. Aggressive investors (age <30, high risk tolerance): 10-20% in crypto. Never exceed 20% - crypto is highly volatile! Recommended 2026 portfolio structure: 40% Equity (stocks/mutual funds), 25% Debt (bonds/FDs), 15% Real estate, 10% Gold, 5-10% Crypto, 5% Emergency cash. Rules: 1) Invest only surplus money you can afford to lose, 2) Never take loan for crypto, 3) Don't invest emergency fund, 4) Build traditional assets first, then add crypto, 5) Rebalance annually - if crypto grows to 25%, book profits. Crypto is satellite position, not core holding.

Timing crypto market is nearly impossible - even experts fail. Best approach: Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA). Instead of lumpsum ‚Çπ1 lakh, invest ‚Çπ10,000 monthly for 10 months.
