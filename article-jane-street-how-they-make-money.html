ETF arbitrage is Jane Street's crown jewel. This is where they became kings, and most people don't even understand what it means.

01

What Is an ETF?

An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a basket of stocks bundled into one tradable unit. For example, the SPY ETF contains all 500 stocks in the S&P 500. Instead of buying all 500 individually, you just buy one share of SPY. There are ETFs for everything â€” bonds, gold, tech stocks, crypto, Indian markets, you name it.

02

The Price Gap Problem

Here's the thing â€” an ETF has two prices at any given time. There's the market price (what people are actually trading it for) and the NAV price (what the underlying stocks are actually worth). These two prices should be the same. But they're not â€” because supply and demand in the ETF market creates tiny mismatches.

Example: SPY trades at $502.50 on the exchange, but the 500 stocks inside it are actually worth $502.55. That $0.05 gap = free money for someone fast enough.
03

How Jane Street Exploits the Gap

When the ETF trades below its NAV: Jane Street buys the cheap ETF shares and simultaneously sells the underlying stocks. Instant profit.

When the ETF trades above its NAV: Jane Street buys the underlying stocks, creates new ETF shares (yes, they can literally create and destroy ETF shares as an "Authorized Participant"), and sells them at the higher price.

Key point: This trade is essentially risk-free. They're buying and selling the same thing in two different forms. The profit is locked in before they even place the order.
04

Why Only Jane Street Can Do This

The gaps are microscopic â€” often less than $0.01. And they last for milliseconds. To capture them, you need:

1. Supercomputers that can calculate the NAV of hundreds of ETFs in real-time.
2. Direct market access to dozens of exchanges simultaneously.
3. Algorithms that execute trades in microseconds.
4. "Authorized Participant" status with ETF issuers (only ~50 firms have this globally).
5. Enough capital to buy millions of dollars of stocks in an instant.

Scale: Jane Street is the largest ETF market maker in the world. They handle a significant percentage of all ETF trading globally. That's a LOT of tiny spreads adding up.
