Understanding Expense Ratio (TER):

Total Expense Ratio (TER) is the annual fee charged by mutual funds for fund management, operations, and other expenses. It's deducted from the fund's NAV daily. Example: If TER is 0.50%, you pay â‚¹500/year on â‚¹1 lakh investment. Impact: 1% TER on â‚¹10L growing at 12% for 30 years = â‚¹2.4 Cr vs â‚¹3.3 Cr (0% TER) - you lose â‚¹90 lakhs! Even 0.1% difference compounds massively. For Nifty 50 funds, all track same index - only TER differentiates returns. SEBI capped TER: 1% for equity funds >â‚¹500 Cr AUM, 2.25% for smaller funds. Frequently Asked Questions

What is expense ratio and how does it affect my returns?

Expense ratio (TER - Total Expense Ratio) is the annual fee charged by mutual funds, expressed as % of assets. It covers fund management, administration, marketing, and operational costs. Impact: TER is deducted from NAV daily, reducing your returns. Example: â‚¹1 lakh invested at 12% return with 0.5% TER = 11.5% net return. Over 30 years: 0% TER = â‚¹29.96L, 0.5% TER = â‚¹24.16L (â‚¹5.8L difference!), 1% TER = â‚¹19.42L (â‚¹10.54L difference!). Why it matters: Small % differences compound massively over time. 0.1% higher TER can cost lakhs over decades. For index funds tracking same index, TER is the ONLY differentiator. Always choose lowest TER fund in same category. Direct plans have 0.5-1% lower TER than Regular plans - prefer Direct.

What is a good expense ratio for index funds in India?

Excellent: 0.05-0.15% (Ultra-low cost like Navi Nifty 50 0.06%, Nippon India Nifty 50 0.06%). Good: 0.15-0.30% (Most quality index funds). Average: 0.30-0.50% (Acceptable but not optimal). High: >0.50% (Avoid unless special index). Direct vs Regular: Direct plans: 0.05-0.30%, Regular plans: 0.50-1.00% (avoid Regular - 0.5-0.7% extra cost for no benefit). International funds: 0.30-0.80% acceptable (higher due to overseas investing costs). Index fund average in India: ~0.25% for Direct Nifty 50/Sensex funds. Comparison: US index funds like Vanguard charge 0.03-0.05%, India is catching up. Top picks 2026: Navi Nifty 50 (0.06%), Nippon Nifty 50 (0.06%), ICICI Nifty 50 (0.10%), UTI Nifty 50 (0.10%). Avoid funds >0.30% TER unless tracking specialized indices.

YES, absolutely! Regular plans have 0.5-1% higher TER (distributor commission). Over 20+ years, this costs 20-30% of your wealth. Why do some index funds have higher expense ratios than others?

Factors affecting TER: 1) AUM (Assets Under Management): Larger funds spread fixed costs over more assets = lower TER. Navi/Nippon have huge AUM = 0.06%. Smaller funds: 0.30-0.50%. 2) AMC efficiency: Some AMCs optimize operations better. 3) Distribution costs: Regular plans pay 0.5-1% to distributors. Direct plans don't. 4) Fund house strategy: Some AMCs deliberately price aggressively to gain market share. 5) Index complexity: Simple indices (Nifty 50) = lower costs. Complex indices (Multi-factor, Smart Beta) = higher costs. 6) Overseas investing: International index funds have higher TER (0.50-0.80%) due to foreign transaction costs. SEBI caps: Max TER: Equity funds >â‚¹500Cr AUM = 1.05%, >â‚¹750Cr = 1.00%, >â‚¹1000Cr = 0.95% (declining slab). Reality: For same index (e.g., Nifty 50), funds differ ONLY in TER and tracking error. Performance is identical. Choose lowest TER always.
