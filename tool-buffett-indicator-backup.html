üéì Why Warren Buffett Loves This Indicator

Warren Buffett called this ratio "probably the best single measure of where valuations stand at any given moment" in a 2001 Fortune Magazine article. Here's why it's so powerful:

  • Simple & Reliable: Compares stock market value to actual economic output
  • Mean Reversion: Historically, extreme readings have corrected themselves
  • Long-term View: Best used for multi-year investment decisions, not short-term trading
  • Context Matters: Consider interest rates, global events, and market structure changes
  • Historical Track Record: Accurately signaled the 2000 and 2008 crashes when ratio exceeded 140%

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Limitations

  • Doesn't predict short-term market movements
  • Can stay "overvalued" or "undervalued" for extended periods
  • Interest rates affect fair value levels (low rates = higher sustainable ratios)
  • Globalization means US companies earn more overseas revenue than before
  • Use as one tool among many - not a standalone signal for buying/selling
