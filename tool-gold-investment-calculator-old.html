Physical Gold vs Gold ETF vs SGB - Which is best in 2026?

Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB) are the best for long-term investment (5+ years): Tax-free capital gains if held till maturity (8 years), 2.5% annual interest on investment, no storage costs, no making charges, demat form. Gold ETF wins for flexibility: Easy to buy/sell on stock exchange, liquid (sell anytime), low expense ratio (0.5%), no storage hassles, ideal for 3-5 year horizon. Physical Gold suits for: Wedding/family needs, emotional/cultural value, immediate possession, but has making charges (10-18%), storage costs, risk of theft. Best strategy 2026: 60% SGB (long-term), 30% Gold ETF (medium-term), 10% physical gold (emergency/occasions).

Gold ETF vs Gold Mutual Fund - which has better returns?

Both track gold prices, minor differences: Gold ETF: Trades on stock exchange like stocks, need demat + trading account, buy/sell at live market price throughout trading hours, expense ratio: 0.5-1%, more transparent pricing. Gold Fund of Funds (Mutual Fund): Invests in Gold ETFs, no demat account needed (direct mutual fund), buy/sell at NAV (end of day), expense ratio: 0.8-1.2% (slightly higher), easier for beginners. Returns: Almost identical since both track physical gold. Gold ETF slightly better due to lower expenses (0.2-0.3% annual difference = ‚Çπ200-300 on ‚Çπ1 lakh). Choose Gold ETF if: You have demat account, want intraday trading flexibility, prefer slightly lower costs. Choose Gold MF if: No demat account, want SIP facility (systematic investment), prefer simplicity. Performance difference is minimal - both deliver ~8-10% annually matching gold price appreciation. Key: Hold for long term (5+ years) to benefit from gold's wealth preservation.

Is physical gold or digital gold better for NRIs and Indians?

For NRIs: Digital gold (ETF/SGB) strongly recommended: 1) No overseas shipping hassles, 2) Easy to manage from abroad, 3) Can sell online when in USA/UK, 4) No customs declaration issues during travel, 5) SGB interest credited to Indian bank account. Physical gold problems for NRIs: Declaring to customs (>50g/100g limits), storage in India (family access issues), difficulty selling while abroad. For resident Indians: Digital gold wins for investment, physical for occasions. Digital Gold (ETF/SGB) advantages: No making charges (save 10-18%!), no storage costs/risks, instant liquidity, precise tracking, can invest small amounts (even ‚Çπ500), SIP facility for disciplined accumulation. Physical gold advantages: Cultural/emotional value, wedding gifts, immediate possession, works during digital failures, elderly comfort. Optimal allocation: 80% digital gold (60% SGB + 20% ETF) for wealth creation, 20% physical gold for family needs. Never keep physical gold >‚Çπ5 lakhs at home - bank locker mandatory for security.

What percentage of portfolio should be in gold in 2026?

Aggressive (age <35, high risk): 5-10% in gold - young investors should prioritize equity growth, but some gold for portfolio balance. Ideal 2026 portfolio: 50-60% Equity (stocks/mutual funds), 20-25% Debt (bonds/FDs), 10-15% Gold (SGB/ETF), 5-10% Real estate/alternatives, 5% Emergency cash.
