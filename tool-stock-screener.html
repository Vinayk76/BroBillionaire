Value Investing

Look for stocks with low P/E ratios (< 15), low P/B ratios (< 3), and high dividend yields (> 2%). Warren Buffett's favorite strategy!

Growth Investing

Focus on high revenue growth (> 20%), profit growth (> 15%), and strong ROE (> 15%). These companies are expanding rapidly.

Quality Stocks

Combine low debt (D/E < 1), high ROE (> 15%), and consistent profit growth. These are stable, financially sound companies.

Momentum Trading

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find undervalued stocks?
Use the "Value Stocks" preset or set P/E ratio below 15, P/B ratio below 3, and look for dividend yields above 2%. Also check that debt-to-equity is below 1 for safety.
What are good fundamental ratios?
Generally, ROE above 15%, P/E between 10-20, Debt/Equity below 1, and consistent revenue/profit growth above 15% indicate strong fundamentals. But always compare within the same sector.
How to find dividend stocks?
Set dividend yield minimum to 3-4%, ensure low debt (D/E < 1), and check for consistent profit growth. Companies with 10+ years of dividend history are best.
What is a good P/E ratio?
P/E between 15-25 is generally fair for most sectors. Below 15 may indicate undervaluation, while above 30 suggests overvaluation. Always compare within the same industry.
How important is ROE?
ROE above 15% is excellent, showing efficient use of shareholder capital. Top companies maintain ROE above 20%. However, very high ROE (>30%) may indicate high financial leverage.
Should I avoid high debt companies?
Generally yes, especially for beginners. Debt-to-Equity above 2 is risky. However, some capital-intensive industries (infrastructure, telecom) naturally have higher debt. Always analyze in sector context.
