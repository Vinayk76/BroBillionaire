Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Iron Condor and when should I use it?

Iron Condor = Bear Call Spread + Bull Put Spread. You sell an OTM call and put while buying further OTM calls/puts for protection. This creates a defined-risk, defined-reward strategy perfect for range-bound markets. Use when: 1) Market has been consolidating for weeks, 2) IV Rank is high (>60) so premiums are juicy, 3) No major events on calendar, 4) 30-45 days to expiry for optimal theta decay. Avoid during: Strong trends, low IV, event weeks (budget, RBI policy), earnings season. Iron condor profits from time decay and decreasing volatility - you want market to stay still and premiums to shrink.

How do I choose strikes for Iron Condor?

Strike selection strategy: 1) Sell options at 0.20-0.30 delta (70-80% probability of expiring OTM), 2) Buy wings 50-100 points away for protection, 3) Keep wings equidistant for balanced risk. Example for Nifty at 19500: Sell 19300 put, Buy 19200 put, Sell 19700 call, Buy 19800 call. Wider wings = lower risk but lower credit. Narrower wings = higher credit but higher risk. Target 1:3 risk-reward minimum. Professional setup: 100-point wide spreads in Nifty, 300-500 point spreads in Bank Nifty. Adjust based on volatility - wider spreads when IV high, tighter when IV low.

What's a good return on risk for Iron Condor?

Good return on risk (credit / max loss): 25-40% = Excellent (high probability, decent credit), 15-25% = Acceptable (standard iron condor), Below 15% = Poor (too much risk for small credit). How do I manage an Iron Condor position?

Iron Condor management rules: 1) 50% Profit Rule: Close position when you've captured 50% of max credit. Don't be greedy. 2) 100% Loss Rule: Exit if loss reaches 100% of credit received. Don't let it go to max loss. 3) Tested Side: If price approaches one breakeven, close that side or roll it further OTM. Leave profitable side open. 4) Time Rule: Exit 1-2 weeks before expiry even if not profitable - gamma risk accelerates. 5) Adjustment: If challenged, convert to inverted strangle or butterfly. Advanced: Track theta daily - if not collecting expected time decay, something's wrong. Monitor IV changes - sudden spike can hurt all short options.

Can I trade Iron Condor every week?

Yes, but with discipline. Weekly iron condors work well when: 1) Market is in consolidation phase, 2) High IV provides good premiums, 3) You follow strict rules. Strategy: Enter Monday/Tuesday (30-45 days out), exit Thursday/Friday or at 50% profit. Don't hold through expiry. Position sizing critical: Never risk more than 2-5% capital per condor. Run multiple small condors rather than one large position. Track overall portfolio delta and gamma - don't get too directionally exposed. During volatile periods or trending markets, skip weeks. Professional traders deploy iron condors as part of portfolio - not sole strategy. Combine with directional trades and other strategies for balanced approach.
