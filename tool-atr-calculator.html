üìà What is ATR (Average True Range)?

ATR is a technical analysis indicator that measures market volatility by decomposing the entire range of an asset price for a given period. Developed by J. Welles Wilder, ATR is commonly used for setting stop-loss levels.

ATR Formula

True Range = Max[(High - Low), |High - Previous Close|, |Low - Previous Close|]

ATR = (Previous ATR √ó (n-1) + Current TR) / n

How to Use ATR for Stop Loss

  • 1x ATR: Tight stop, higher chance of being stopped out, used in strong trends
  • 1.5x ATR: Moderate stop, good for swing trading
  • 2x ATR: Standard stop, gives room for normal volatility
  • 3x ATR: Wide stop, used in highly volatile markets

Best ATR Settings

  • Day Trading: 7-10 period ATR on 5-15 min charts
  • Swing Trading: 14 period ATR on daily charts (default)
  • Position Trading: 14-21 period ATR on weekly charts
