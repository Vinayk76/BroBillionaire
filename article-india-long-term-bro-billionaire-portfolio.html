Rebalancing Strategy

Why rebalance: Winners keep winning (Nvidia 10x), losers stay flat (Coinbase). Your50-50 portfolio becomes 90-10. Rebalancing locks profits and maintains risk levels.

Annual Rebalancing (Recommended)

Frequency: Once per year (e.g., every December)

Process:

  1. Check each stock's % of US portfolio
  2. If any stock >35%, trim to 25% (sell 10%)
  3. If any stock <5%, either top up or exit completely
  4. Reinvest trimmed profits into underweight stocks

Tax-Optimized Execution

Hold >24 Months for LTCG

STCG (<24 months): Taxed as per slab (up to 30%)
LTCG (>24 months): 20% + indexation = ~15-18% effective tax

Strategy: When rebalancing, prioritize selling positions you've held >24 months. Minimize STCG hits.

Tax-Loss Harvesting

If Coinbase is down 30%, sell before March 31 to realize loss. Offset against Nvidia gains. Reduces tax liability.

Wash-sale workaround: Sell Coinbase, wait 30 days, buy back. Or buy a similar crypto stock (e.g., Robinhood) immediately.

Exit Strategy: When to Trim/Sell

Rule #1: Never Exit 100% on Fundamental Thesis

If you bought Nvidia for AI monopoly and that's intact, never sell all of it. Trim on valuation spikes, but keep core position.

Rule #2: Trim on Parabolic Moves

Trigger: Stock +200% in 12 months (e.g., Nvidia Jan '25: $500 ‚Üí Jan '26: $1,500)

Action: Sell 30-50% ‚Üí Lock massive profits, let rest ride.

Rule #3: Exit if Thesis Breaks

Examples of broken thesis:

  • Tesla: FSD fails completely, competition destroys market share, Elon leaves
  • Nvidia: AMD/Intel steal 50% market share, AI spending crashes
  • Coinbase: Crypto banned globally, platform hacked

If fundamental story breaks, exit completely. Don't hope for recovery.

Rule #4: Lifecycle-Based Trim

Age 55-60: Start reducing high-volatility stocks (Tesla, Palantir). Shift to stable dividend payers (Apple, Microsoft).

Age 60-65: Reduce US exposure from 30% to 15-20%. Increase debt allocation.

Retirement (65+): Keep 10% in US blue chips for inflation hedge. Move rest to bonds/FDs.

Risk Management

Drawdown Tolerance

Expect 30-50% crashes. Tesla dropped 70% in 2022. If you can't stomach that, reduce allocation or stick to safer stocks (Microsoft, Apple).

Position Sizing

  • No single stock >30% of US portfolio (prevents one blowup from wiping you out)
  • High-risk stocks (Palantir, Coinbase) <10% each
  • Core positions (Nvidia, Microsoft) 20-30% each

Liquidity Reserve

Keep 6-12 months expenses in Indian bank/liquid funds. Never sell US stocks in emergency. They're for 10+ year wealth, not short-term cash needs.
