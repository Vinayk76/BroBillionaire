üéØ How to Compare Mutual Funds

Choosing the right mutual fund requires careful analysis of multiple factors. Our comparison tool helps you evaluate funds side-by-side to make informed investment decisions.

Key Factors to Compare

  • Returns (CAGR): Historical compound annual growth rate shows consistent performance
  • Expense Ratio: Lower expenses mean higher net returns for investors
  • Total Returns: Final corpus after investment period shows actual wealth creation
  • Risk Profile: Standard deviation and volatility indicate investment safety
  • Fund Manager: Track record and consistency of fund management team

SIP vs Lumpsum: Which is Better?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan): Regular monthly investments help average out market volatility and build wealth through rupee cost averaging. Ideal for salaried investors and long-term goals.

Lumpsum: One-time large investment can give higher returns in bull markets but carries timing risk. Better when you have a windfall or markets are undervalued.

Pro Tip: Don't just chase past returns! Consider expense ratio, fund consistency, and alignment with your investment goals. A fund with 12% CAGR and 0.5% expense ratio may beat a 13% CAGR fund with 1.5% expenses over long term.
