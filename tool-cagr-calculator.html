Understanding CAGR

What is CAGR and why is it important? +
CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is the rate at which an investment would have grown if it had grown at a steady rate. It's the most accurate way to measure investment performance over time because it smooths out volatility and gives you a true annualized return. Unlike absolute returns, CAGR accounts for the time factor, making it perfect for comparing different investments.
How is CAGR different from absolute returns? +
Absolute return shows total percentage gain/loss without considering time. CAGR shows annualized growth rate. Example: If you earned 100% in 5 years, absolute return = 100%, but CAGR = 14.87% per year. CAGR is better for comparing investments with different time periods.
What's a good CAGR for Indian investments? +
Nifty 50: ~12-14% historically | Large cap equity funds: 11-13% | Small/Mid cap funds: 15-18% | FD/Debt funds: 6-8% | Real estate: 8-10% | Gold: 9-11%. Anything beating inflation (6-7%) is decent. Above 15% CAGR consistently is excellent. Remember, higher returns come with higher risk!
