Understanding Stock Valuation Metrics

Warren Buffett built his fortune finding undervalued stocks using fundamental analysis. Here's what each metric tells you:

  • P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings): Shows how much you pay for each dollar of earnings. Lower is generally better. Market average is 15-20x.
  • P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book): Compares price to book value. Below 1.0 means trading below net assets - potential value play!
  • P/S Ratio (Price-to-Sales): Price relative to revenue. Useful for growth stocks or companies with temporary losses.
  • Industry Comparison: Always compare to sector peers. Tech stocks trade higher than utilities - that's normal!
  • Growth Matters: High-growth companies deserve premium valuations. Don't just chase low P/E blindly.

Important Limitations & Warnings

  • Valuation isn't everything: A cheap stock can get cheaper if the business is deteriorating
  • Quality matters: Sometimes expensive stocks are worth it (Apple, Microsoft prove this)
  • Growth justifies premiums: Fast-growing companies deserve higher P/E ratios
  • Check the business: Read financial statements, understand the competitive moat, assess management
  • Value traps exist: Low P/E doesn't always mean bargain - could signal dying business
  • This is a starting point: Use this tool for initial screening, then do deep research before investing
  • Market sentiment matters: "Undervalued" stocks can stay undervalued during bear markets
