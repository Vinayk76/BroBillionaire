What is CGST, SGST and IGST? ‚ñº
CGST (Central GST): Tax collected by Central Government for intra-state sales.
SGST (State GST): Tax collected by State Government for intra-state sales.
IGST (Integrated GST): Tax for inter-state sales, collected by Central Government and shared with destination state.
For intra-state: 18% GST = 9% CGST + 9% SGST. For inter-state: 18% GST = 18% IGST.
When to use Intra-State vs Inter-State GST? ‚ñº
Intra-State (CGST + SGST): When buyer and seller are in the same state. E.g., Seller in Maharashtra sells to buyer in Maharashtra.
Inter-State (IGST): When buyer and seller are in different states. E.g., Seller in Maharashtra sells to buyer in Karnataka.
What items have 0% GST? ‚ñº
Items exempted from GST (0% rate) include: Fresh fruits and vegetables, Milk, Bread, Salt, Natural honey, Fresh meat and fish, Eggs, Educational services, Healthcare services, Agricultural services, and Public transportation.
