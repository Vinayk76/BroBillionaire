Best Cashback Cards

Cashback cards offer straightforward rewards in the form of statement credits or direct cashback. Unlike reward points that may have complex redemption rules, cashback provides clear, tangible value without the need for point calculations or transfer partners.

When Cashback Makes Sense

Cashback cards can be more suitable for those who don't frequently redeem travel rewards. Benefits include: no point devaluation risk, simple automatic crediting, and no restrictions on when you can use the value earned. Consider your spending patterns when deciding between cashback and reward-based cards.

Best Cards for Beginners

Entry-level credit cards are designed for those new to credit or with limited credit history. These cards typically have lower income requirements, minimal or zero annual fees, and straightforward reward structures.

Building Credit History

When starting with credit cards, focus on: (1) Low or zero annual fee to minimize costs, (2) Reasonable eligibility requirements, (3) Consistent on-time payments to build credit history. After 2+ years of responsible credit usage, you may qualify for premium card upgrades.

Best Student Credit Cards

Student credit cards are designed for college students and young adults with limited or no credit history. They offer lower credit limits, minimal or no annual fees, and help students build credit history early while providing convenience for online purchases and emergencies.

Building Credit as a Student

Start with a low-limit card, always pay in full, and use less than 30% of your credit limit. After graduating and getting a job, your early credit history will help you qualify for premium cards with better rewards and higher limits.

How to Choose Your Card

Choosing a credit card isn't about getting the "best" card it's about getting the right card for YOUR spending patterns. Here's the framework:

If You Travel Internationally 3+ Times/Year...

Go for Axis Atlas. The 5% international rewards + no forex markup will save you more than any other card. Add HDFC Diners Black for domestic travel.

For Online Shopping Focus

Amazon Pay ICICI (free) + Axis Flipkart (500/year) provides 5% rewards on both major e-commerce platforms.

For Simplified Cashback

HSBC Cashback offers 1.5% on all purchases, unlimited, auto-credited monthly. No categories to track, no points to redeem.

For High Annual Spending (12L+)

HDFC Infinia offers the highest consistent reward rate at 3.3% on all spends, though it typically requires an upgrade path from HDFC Regalia First in 2026. Minimum income requirement is now 12L per annum.

For Business Owners

HDFC BizBlack offers the same 3.3% rewards as Diners Black with GST-compliant invoicing and employee add-on cards. For startups, SBI SimplySAVE Business provides 10X on office expenses at just 499/year.

For Ultra-HNI / Invite-Only Cards

Limited edition cards like Amex Centurion, HDFC Infinia Metal, and ICICI Emeralde Priv are invite-only. Build relationship with your bank through high spends (60L+/year for Amex, 12L+/year for HDFC) and NRV (7Cr+ for ICICI) to receive an invitation in 2026. These cards offer exclusive experiences money alone cannot buy.

For Students

Studying abroad? ICICI Student Travel Card or HDFC ForexPlus with zero forex markup. In India? SBI Student Plus is your first step to building credit with no income proof required.

To Build/Repair Credit Score

SBI Unnati (secured card, free for 4 years) or HDFC MoneyBack+ Secured are guaranteed approval cards backed by FD (minimum 30,000 FD in 2026). After 12 months of timely payments, you can upgrade to regular unsecured cards.

Important Considerations

  • Carrying a balance Credit card interest rates (36-42% APR) can significantly exceed any rewards earned
  • Multiple applications Applying for many cards in a short period may impact your credit score
  • Annual fee evaluation Ensure the rewards and benefits justify any annual charges
  • Spending behavior Rewards should complement existing spending, not encourage additional purchases
  • Payment timeliness Late payments affect both credit score and incur additional charges
