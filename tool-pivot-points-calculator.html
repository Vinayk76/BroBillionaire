Frequently Asked Questions

What are pivot points and how are they used in trading?

Pivot points are technical indicators that use the previous day's high, low, and close prices to calculate support and resistance levels for the current trading session. Traders use these levels to identify potential entry/exit points, set stop losses, and determine intraday price targets for stocks, indices, and commodities.

Which pivot point method is most accurate?

Classic pivot points are the most widely used and tested. However, Camarilla pivots work well for intraday trading with tight ranges, while Fibonacci pivots are popular for swing trading. The best method depends on your trading style, timeframe, and the market you're trading. Try multiple methods and see which aligns best with actual price action.

Can I use pivot points for Bank Nifty and Nifty intraday trading?

Yes, pivot points are extremely popular for Bank Nifty and Nifty intraday trading. Many professional traders use them to identify key levels for options buying/selling. Enter yesterday's high, low, and close prices to calculate today's support and resistance levels. Camarilla pivots are particularly effective for Indian index trading.

How do I trade using pivot points?

Buy near support levels (S1, S2, S3) when price shows reversal signals, with stop loss below next support. Sell near resistance levels (R1, R2, R3) when price shows weakness. If price breaks above pivot point with volume, it's bullish; below pivot is bearish. Use pivot points with candlestick patterns, volume, and momentum indicators for confirmation.

What's the difference between Classic and Camarilla pivots?

Classic pivots use simple averaging of high, low, and close to calculate levels. Camarilla pivots place more emphasis on the close price and generate tighter support/resistance levels (up to 4 levels on each side), making them ideal for intraday range trading and scalping. Camarilla levels are particularly accurate in sideways markets.

Do pivot points work for all timeframes?

Pivot points work best for intraday trading using daily data. You can also calculate weekly pivots for swing trading (using last week's data) or monthly pivots for positional trading. The most popular application is daily pivots for intraday trading in stocks, futures, and options markets.
