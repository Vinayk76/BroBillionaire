Frequently Asked Questions

When should I use Straddle vs Strangle?

Use Straddle when expecting huge volatility and willing to pay higher premium - both options ATM give maximum sensitivity to moves but cost more. Use Strangle when you want cheaper entry - both OTM options cost less but need larger moves to profit. Long Straddle: Best before major events (budget, RBI policy) when you're certain of big move but not direction. Costs ₹200-300 for Nifty ATM typically. Long Strangle: Best when IV is low and you want lower capital commitment. Costs ₹100-150 but needs 3-4% move minimum. Straddle profits faster, Strangle needs more patience but risks less.

What's the best time to buy a Long Straddle?

Buy Long Straddle when: 1) IV Rank below 30 (premiums cheap), 2) 1-2 days before major event (budget, policy, earnings), 3) During unusual calm before expected move, 4) When market is consolidating near key levels. Best example: India VIX at 12, budget in 2 days - buy Nifty ATM straddle. Exit immediately after event when IV collapses. Don't hold past 15 days - theta kills you. Wrong time: High IV (VIX > 20), no catalysts ahead, expiry week (gamma risk high). Calculate if you can profit: if straddle costs ₹250, you need 250/50 = ₹5 move per lot just to breakeven daily from theta.

Is Short Straddle safe? When should I use it?

Short Straddle is HIGH RISK - unlimited loss potential both sides. Only for experienced traders with: 1) Strong risk management, 2) Ability to adjust/hedge, 3) Adequate capital for margin. Safe time to sell: IV Rank > 80 (premiums bloated), range-bound market for weeks, post-event IV collapse, 30-45 days to expiry (optimal theta collection). Never sell straddle before events or in trending markets. Use defined-risk alternatives (Iron Condor, Credit Spreads) if you're not expert. Professional approach: Sell straddle, immediately set stop loss at 2x premium received. Exit at 50% profit or 100% loss. Monitor daily. Hedge with futures if move accelerates.

How do I manage a Straddle position?

Long Straddle management: 1) Exit one leg if it's losing (market moving one direction), let winner run, 2) Roll to next expiry if trade needs more time, 3) Exit at 100% profit or 50% loss, 4) Book profits immediately after IV spike. Don't wait for max profit. Short Straddle management: 1) Set alerts at breakeven points, 2) If breached, roll losing leg further OTM, 3) Consider converting to Iron Condor by buying wings, 4) Exit at 50% profit - don't be greedy, 5) Close immediately if loss reaches 100% of premium received. Never hold short straddle into expiry week - gamma risk explodes. Advanced: Adjust delta to neutral if market moves, convert to directional trade if conviction changes.

What's the minimum move needed for Long Straddle to profit?

Calculate breakeven: Strike + Total Premium = Upper Breakeven. Strike - Total Premium = Lower Breakeven. Example: Nifty 19500 straddle costs ₹240 (₹120 call + ₹120 put). Upper BE = 19500 + 240 = 19740. Lower BE = 19500 - 240 = 19260. Need 1.23% move minimum. But this is at expiry! During position life, you need less move if IV expands. If buying 7 days before expiry, need at least 2% move due to theta decay. Rule of thumb: Straddle needs 1.5-2% move in Nifty, 2-3% in Bank Nifty to show profit after factoring time decay. Higher IV = higher breakeven. Calculate weekly: if theta is ₹35/week, underlying must move more than that to profit.
