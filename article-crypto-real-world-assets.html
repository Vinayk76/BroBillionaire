What Are Real World Assets?

Real World Assets (RWAs) are traditional financial and physical assets that are tokenized and represented on a blockchain. Instead of paper deeds, stock certificates, or bond documents, ownership is recorded on-chain as tokens.

Risks to Consider

‚öñÔ∏è
Regulatory Uncertainty
Securities laws vary by country. What's legal in one jurisdiction may not be in another. Regulatory clarity is still evolving.
üèõÔ∏è
Counterparty Risk
Tokens are only as good as the issuer/custodian. If they fail or defraud, your on-chain token may be worthless.
üîê
Smart Contract Risk
Bugs in tokenization contracts could affect assets. Audits help but don't eliminate risk entirely.
üíß
Liquidity Risk
Secondary markets may be thin. You expect to not be able to sell when you want at the price you expect.
üìú
Legal Enforcement
Blockchain ownership ‚â† legal ownership. Verify the legal structure. Can you actually enforce your rights?
üîë
KYC/Access Barriers
Most RWAs require KYC and may be restricted to accredited investors or specific jurisdictions.

Due Diligence Checklist

Before investing in any RWA: Verify the legal structure, check if assets are audited, understand redemption process, confirm regulatory compliance, and only use established platforms.

How to Get Started

  1. Start with Treasury Products ‚Äî Lowest risk. Ondo's USDY or Franklin Templeton's BENJI are good starting points
  2. Complete KYC ‚Äî Most RWA platforms require identity verification
  3. Small Test First ‚Äî Try the full process (deposit, earn, withdraw) with small amounts
  4. Understand the Structure ‚Äî Is it a security? What's the legal wrapper? Who's the custodian?
  5. Diversify ‚Äî Don't put everything in one platform or asset type
  6. Track Yields ‚Äî RWA returns are typically paid regularly ‚Äî understand the schedule

RWAs bridge the $400 trillion traditional finance world to blockchain. Early adopters who understand this space will have a massive head start.
