#1: Vested Finance ‚Äî Best for Beginners

Verdict: If you're new to US investing, start here. Zero commission, fractional shares (buy 0.01 Tesla), clean mobile app, and Indian rupee interface.

Key Features:

  • Zero commission on US stocks (Vested makes money from forex markup ~0.5%)
  • Fractional shares: Buy $10 worth of any stock (great for Tesla at $200+)
  • Partner: DriveWealth (US broker-dealer, FDIC insured up to $250K)
  • Tax support: Pre-filled ITR data, integrates with Form 26AS
  • Minimum: ‚Çπ1,000 to start
  • Withdrawal fee: $5 per transfer back to India

Pros: Easiest onboarding, mobile-first, great educational content, SIP-style recurring investments available.

Cons: No options trading, limited research tools, forex markup adds up on large trades.

Best Use Case

You want to start with ‚Çπ10,000-50,000/month, buy 2-3 Bro Billionaire Stocks, and learn. Vested is perfect.

Verdict: If you want a beautiful, intuitive app that handles both Indian stocks + US stocks in one place, INDMoney wins.

  • Zero commission on US stocks

#3: Interactive Brokers (IBKR) ‚Äî Best for Pros

Verdict: For experienced traders who want professional tools, options/futures access, and ultra-low margin rates. Not for beginners.

Key Features:

  • Commission: Tiered ($0.0035/share, min $0.35) or Fixed ($0.005/share, min $1). Tiny for large trades.
  • Options + Futures: Trade options on TSLA, NVDA. Sell covered calls, buy protective puts.
  • Margin: Borrow at 3-6% (cheapest in industry). Leverage your portfolio.
  • Global markets: Access 150+ markets (not just US‚Äîbuy European, Asian stocks too)
  • Research tools: Best-in-class charts, scanners, algo trading APIs

Pros: Professional-grade, cheapest for high-volume traders, advanced strategies possible.

Cons: Steep learning curve, intimidating UI, small per-trade fees hurt tiny investments (~$100).

Best Use Case

You have ‚Çπ10+ lakh to invest, want to trade options, or need margin/leverage. IBKR is the institutional-grade choice.
