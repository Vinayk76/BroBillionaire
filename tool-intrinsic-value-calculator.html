üí° Valuation Note

DCF is highly sensitive to assumptions. Small changes in growth or discount rate dramatically impact valuation. Use margin of safety (buy at 30-40% below intrinsic value) to account for uncertainty.

DCF Formula
Sum of (FCF √ó (1+g)^n / (1+r)^n) for 10 years + Terminal Value / (1+r)^10. Then subtract debt, add cash, divide by shares. Complex but most accurate valuation method for cash-generative businesses.
üí∞
Free Cash Flow
FCF = Operating Cash Flow - CapEx. Find in cash flow statement. Use 3-year average for stability. Ignore one-time items. Positive & growing FCF = healthy business. Negative FCF = avoid or investigate deeply.
üìà
Growth Rate Assumptions
Use historical 5-10 year FCF CAGR as baseline. Adjust for industry prospects, competitive moat, management quality. Be conservative! 15%+ growth for 10 years is rare. Most companies can't sustain >10% forever.

Margin of Safety
Never buy at calculated fair value. Buy at 60-70% of intrinsic value (30-40% MOS). Accounts for model errors, unforeseen risks, human bias. Warren Buffett's golden rule for value investing.

DCF Limitations
Doesn't work for: Banks (use P/B), cyclical stocks, loss-making companies, asset-light tech. Sensitive to assumptions - 2% change in discount rate = 20-30% valuation swing. Use alongside other valuation methods.
