Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good credit score in India and why does it matter?

Credit score ranges in India: 300-549 = Poor (loan rejection), 550-649 = Fair (high interest rates), 650-749 = Good (moderate rates), 750-900 = Excellent (best rates, instant approval). A score above 750 is considered ideal. Impact: 750+ score saves lakhs in interest over loan lifetime. Example: ‚Çπ50 lakh home loan at 8.5% vs 9.5% = ‚Çπ8+ lakh difference over 20 years. Higher scores also mean: Instant credit card approvals, Higher credit limits, Pre-approved loan offers, Better negotiating power, Lower insurance premiums (in some cases).

What is credit utilization and how does it affect my score?

Credit utilization = (Total credit card balance / Total credit limit) √ó 100. Example: ‚Çπ1.5 lakh used of ‚Çπ5 lakh limit = 30% utilization. Impact on score: <10% utilization = Excellent (adds 30+ points), 10-30% = Good, 30-50% = Average (neutral), 50-70% = High (reduces 20-30 points), >70% = Very high (reduces 50+ points, signals financial stress). How to improve: 1) Pay dues before statement generation, 2) Request credit limit increase, 3) Use multiple cards to spread utilization, 4) Make multiple payments per month. This factor contributes 30% to your credit score - second most important after payment history.

How quickly can I improve my credit score from 650 to 750?

Timeline to improve score by 100 points: Fast track (3-6 months): 1) Reduce utilization to <10% immediately (+30 pts), 2) Pay all bills on time for 6 months (+25 pts), 3) Clear any payment defaults (+30 pts), 4) Dispute credit report errors (+15 pts). Total: 100 points achievable in 6 months with aggressive action. Normal track (6-12 months): Consistent on-time payments, gradual utilization reduction, no new credit applications. Slow track (12-24 months): If you have serious defaults, settlements, or bankruptcies. Key: Payment history accounts for 35% of score. Even one 30-day late payment can drop score by 50-100 points. Focus on zero defaults first.

Does checking my own credit score lower it?

No! Checking your own credit score is a "soft inquiry" and does NOT affect your score. Check as often as you want on CIBIL, Experian, or free platforms. What DOES affect score: "Hard inquiries" - when banks/lenders check your score for loan/credit card applications. Each hard inquiry reduces score by 5-10 points. Multiple inquiries in short period (30 days) signal credit hunger to lenders. Smart strategy: Check your score monthly to track progress, Space out loan applications by 6+ months, Apply for credit only when necessary, Use rate shopping window - Multiple applications for same loan type within 14-45 days count as single inquiry. Dispute any unauthorized hard inquiries on your report.

Will closing old credit cards improve my credit score?

NO! Closing old credit cards usually HURTS your score due to: 1) Reduced total credit limit (increases utilization %), 2) Shorter average credit history age, 3) Lower credit mix diversity. Example: Close ‚Çπ2 lakh limit card ‚Üí Your ‚Çπ1 lakh usage across remaining cards now shows higher utilization. When to close: 1) High annual fee cards you don't use (downgrade to no-fee variant instead), 2) Multiple cards from same bank (consolidate), 3) Lost/stolen cards (report, don't close). When to KEEP: Your oldest credit card (even unused) - contributes to history length. Best practice: Keep 2-3 old cards active with small transactions, Set to autopay, Never close cards unless absolutely necessary.

How do personal loans affect credit score differently than credit cards?

Personal Loan Impact: Immediate: Hard inquiry (-5 to -10 points), New debt added (-10 to -20 points initially). Long-term: Improves credit mix (+15 points), Regular EMI payments boost payment history (+30-50 points), Reduces utilization if used to pay credit cards (+20-40 points). Credit Card Impact: High utilization hurts continuously, Easier to improve score by paying down balance, Revolving credit (more flexible). Strategic use: Take personal loan to consolidate high-interest credit card debt, Maintains payment discipline through fixed EMIs, Total impact after 12 months of timely payments: +50 to +100 points. Warning: Multiple loans simultaneously increases debt burden and can reduce score if EMI/income ratio exceeds 50%.

What happens to credit score during loan foreclosure or settlement?

Foreclosure (paying loan early): POSITIVE impact - Shows financial discipline, No negative remarks on report, Score improves by 10-20 points, Status shows "Closed" (good). Settlement (paying less than owed): VERY NEGATIVE - Marked as "Settled" on report for 7 years, Drops score by 75-100 points immediately, Future lenders see you as high risk, Difficult to get loans for 2-3 years, Higher interest rates even after. Difference is critical: Banks treat foreclosure as positive financial planning, Settlement as inability to pay full amount. Avoid settlement unless extreme financial distress. Better options: Restructure loan, Negotiate lower interest rate, Take debt consolidation loan. Settlement should be absolute last resort only.
