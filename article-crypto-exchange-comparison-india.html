Crypto Trading in India: The Reality

Before choosing an exchange, understand the landscape:

Legal Status

Crypto trading is LEGAL in India. Supreme Court lifted RBI ban in March 2020. No specific regulations yet ‚Äî treated as "virtual digital assets."

Tax Reality

30% flat tax on ALL crypto gains (no offset for losses). 1% TDS on transactions above ‚Çπ10,000. Track every trade for ITR filing.

Banking Issues

Some banks still block crypto transactions. If one fails, try another bank. P2P trading bypasses direct bank integration.

KYC Required

All Indian exchanges require Aadhaar + PAN verification. Takes 1-24 hours. No anonymous trading in India.

Security Best Practices

Enable 2FA

Use Google Authenticator, not SMS. SMS can be SIM-swapped. This is non-negotiable for any exchange.

Don't Keep Everything on Exchange

Exchanges can be hacked or freeze withdrawals. Move large holdings to hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor).

Separate Email

Use a unique email ONLY for crypto exchanges. Makes phishing much harder. Enable 2FA on email too.

Whitelist Withdrawals

Enable withdrawal address whitelist. Only pre-approved addresses can receive funds. Adds 24-hour delay but worth it.
