Step 4: Rebalancing Strategy

Rebalancing = bringing allocations back to target percentages when they drift.

When to Rebalance

Rule: If any stock drifts >5% from target, rebalance.

Example: You started with Nvidia at 14.3%. It rallies to 22% of portfolio. Drift = 7.7%. ‚Üí Trim Nvidia, buy laggards.

Rebalancing Frequency

  • Quarterly (Recommended): Check every 3 months. Rebalance if drift >5%.
  • Semi-Annual: Twice a year. Lower transaction costs, but more drift.
  • Annual: Once a year. Tax-efficient (hold >1 year for LTCG) but allows large drift.

Tax Consideration

India: LTCG (>24 months) = 20% tax vs. STCG (<24 months)=slab rate. Prefer annual rebalancing.

USA: LTCG (>12 months) = 15-20% vs. STCG = income tax rate. Quarterly OK if income low.

Pro Tip: Instead of selling winners, use new capital to buy laggards. Avoids taxes.
