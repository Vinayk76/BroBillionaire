Understanding Real Estate ROI

Total ROI includes rental income + capital appreciation - all expenses. Rental Yield = Annual Rent / Property Value √ó 100. Good rental yield in India: 3-4% residential, 6-8% commercial. Cap Rate = Net Operating Income / Property Value √ó 100. Higher cap rate = better income potential. Cash Flow = Rental Income - (EMI + Expenses). Positive cash flow means property pays for itself. Leverage effect: If ROI > loan rate, leverage magnifies returns. Consider: location appreciation potential, rental demand, liquidity needs, and tax benefits (HRA, Section 24 deductions). Real estate has low liquidity - plan 5+ year holding period. Diversify across asset classes - don't put everything in property.

How much down payment needed for investment property?

Primary residence: 10-20% down payment, 80-90% loan available. Investment property (second home): 25-30% down payment minimum, 70-75% LTV (Loan-to-Value). Commercial property: 30-40% down payment, 60-70% loan. Why higher down payment? Banks consider investment properties riskier - tenant defaults, maintenance issues, market volatility. Loan limits: Combined EMI (all loans) should be <50% of monthly income. Max loan: ‚Çπ75L for metros, ‚Çπ45L for others (varies by bank/income). Strategy tips: 1) Higher down payment = lower EMI = positive cash flow, 2) Min 30% down recommended for comfortable cash flow, 3) Don't stretch finances - keep 6-month emergency fund separate, 4) Consider under-construction property for lower prices but wait 2-3 years. Leverage optimization: If property ROI > loan rate, maximize loan. If uncertain, higher down payment safer.
